Outreach Ministries
Yakama Indian Reservation
More than 8,800 people are enrolled in the Yakama confederation of tribes, and there are more than 13,700 people living on or close by the reservation. Our youth travel there every year to assit with everything from basic grounds keeping to bible study and interaction with the youth that live on the reservation.
Living Assistance Program

Aloha Christian Churches Living Assistance Program touches many in the community giving them that extra little help they need to keep their heat and light on in their home, pay their rent or help with other life expenses and allow them to move towards living with more stability and dignity.

Aloha Christian Church also contributes to Care To Share in the Beaverton/Aloha Area, and Community Action of Washington County.
Week Of Compassion
Week of Compassion is the relief, refugee and development mission fund of the Christian Church (Disciples of Christ). WoC works through implementing partner organizations to facilitate relief and development efforts in areas of great need by providing financial resources. Giving to Week of Compassion is an effective way for Disciples congregations, members and friends to channel their resources and concerns to the humanitarian needs of suffering people within the United States and in the world.
Portland Rescue Mission
In a joint effort with Murray Hills Christian Church, Aloha Christian Church takes part in serving at the Portland Rescue Mission. Serving meals, teaching computer classes, and serving in the warehouse are just some of the ways we serve and minister to the homeless community.
Thank You Ministry
Our Thank You Ministry has been established to let people in the community that they are appreciated for all their hard work. Each week we select a group, (Postal workers, garbage people, social workers, gas station attendents etc) and we send out Thank you cards and trat bags just to let them know we appreciate them and are praying for them.
